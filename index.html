We looked at research about household income (percent per bracket) by state and obesitiy (percent by type - normal, obese, overweight, underweight). When we ran correlation tests between household income with obesity, we found the following:

Normal Obese
< $50K -56.21% 49.89%
$150K + 65.63% -59.09%

For housholds that make less than $50K, we found there to bee a negative correlation to normal body weight, and a positive correlation to being obese. However, we found the exact opposite with households that make more than $150K a year. So, the graphs above show converse trends. This means that there is a relatively high likelyhood that household income (if an individual is on the low or high end) is a key factor in determining weather someone has a normal or an obese BMI.
